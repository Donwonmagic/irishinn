If These Walls Could Talk

From a 1930s roadhouse to a biker bar, and finally, a Glen Echo landmark.

1931

The Fire & The Phoenix

The story begins with a spark. In 1930, the Moxley family home‚Äîwhich stood on this very ground‚Äîburned to the ground. Undeterred, the family rebuilt the structure in 1931 as a tavern to celebrate the end of Prohibition.

1940s

The "Otto's Grill" Era

In 1944, Otto Kumfert purchased the tavern and renamed it Otto‚Äôs Grill. It became a local institution, known for its hearty food and no-nonsense atmosphere.

1986

The Inn at Glen Echo

The rough edges were sanded down in the late 80s when it became The Inn at Glen Echo. The motorcycles were traded for sedans, and the menu shifted toward fine dining.

However, the building still held its secrets. Staff began reporting strange occurrences‚Äîfootsteps in empty rooms, lights flickering, and the feeling of being watched.

The Lady in White

The Inn is widely considered one of the most haunted locations in Maryland. Guests and staff alike have reported seeing a "Lady in White" wandering the upstairs dining rooms. Others tell tales of a mischievous spirit at the bar who likes to reorganize the glassware when no one is looking. Don't worry‚Äîthey seem to be friendly spirits, perhaps just enjoying one last pint.

2003

The Irish Revival

In 2003, Christy Hughes, a native of County Galway and former manager of The Dubliner in D.C., saw the potential in the historic building. Along with his wife Libby, he purchased the property and began a massive renovation.

They peeled back the layers of history to reveal the original wood, added the beautiful oak bar you see today, and reopened as The Irish Inn at Glen Echo.

Today

A Living Landmark

Today, the Inn is a rare blend of upscale dining and neighborhood comfort. It has been named one of the Top 100 Restaurants by Washingtonian and The Washington Post.

Whether you are here for the White Tablecloth dining in the Moxley Room or a pint of Guinness and live music in the pub, you are part of a story that has been 100 years in the making.
